Tokyo

In this imagined future, every person carries a small chip implanted at the back of their head—an ever-present interface between the human mind and the centralized system. To function, individuals must regularly visit designated energy refill stations, where their internal power reserves are replenished.

Daily life becomes a cycle of consumption and recharge, with human productivity finely tuned and sustained by the system. While basic needs are met with precision, the rhythm of life is no longer self-determined—it is regulated, optimized, and monitored.

Shanghai

In the near future, global policies have diverged into two paths: one of decentralized freedom and another of centralized control. This project explores the latter—a world governed by an authoritarian system where technology fulfills every material need, creating a seemingly perfect “Pleasure Garden.”

There is no hunger, no conflict; humanity is gently contained in stability and satisfaction. But as creativity, choice, and freedom fade, can we still call this the future of humanity? This project invites you to step into this utopian virtual world and reflect on its costs and possibilities.

Monster

In pursuit of perfection, a powerful tech conglomerate set out to create the next stage of human evolution—superhumans enhanced with unmatched intelligence, strength, and emotional control. But somewhere along the line, the line between human and machine blurred. What emerged were not heroes, but monsters

beings stripped of empathy, driven by efficiency, and molded by a system that valued control over compassion. These creations now roam the digital and physical world alike, reflections of a vision that forgot to ask: just because we can create them, should we?
